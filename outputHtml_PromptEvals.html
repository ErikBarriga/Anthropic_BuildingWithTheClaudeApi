# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete preparing for an 18km long run session:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (140g), Fat (85g), Carbs (420g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with honey (20g), banana (120g), and whole milk (240g). 1 whole egg scrambled (50g).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 85g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** White bread (80g) with peanut butter (25g) and jam (15g). Orange juice (250ml).
    * Protein: 10g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 65g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Grilled chicken breast (150g), white rice (150g cooked), steamed carrots (100g), and olive oil (10ml).
    * Protein: 45g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 75g

* **Pre-Run Snack (3:30 PM - 30 minutes before run):** Energy bar (65g) and sports drink (500ml).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 85g

* **Post-Run Recovery Snack (5:00 PM - within 30 minutes of finishing):** Chocolate milk (500ml) with a banana (100g).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 70g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Lean ground beef (150g), whole wheat pasta (120g cooked), tomato sauce (150g), and parmesan cheese (20g).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 20g, Carbs: 65g

* **Evening Snack (9:00 PM):** Greek yogurt (200g) with granola (40g) and berries (80g).
    * Protein: 16g, Fat: 13g, Carbs: 45g

This meal plan emphasizes carbohydrate-loading for sustained energy during the long run, adequate protein for muscle recovery, and strategic timing of meals and snacks to optimize performance and recovery.
